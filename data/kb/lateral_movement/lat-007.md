SMB share enumeration identifies accessible network shares that may contain sensitive files, credentials, scripts, or configuration data useful for lateral movement and privilege escalation. Tools like `smbclient`, `enum4linux`, `smbmap`, and CrackMapExec can list shares, enumerate users and groups, check for null session access, and recursively spider accessible share contents for interesting files. Finding world-readable shares containing backup files, scripts with hardcoded credentials, or SYSVOL/NETLOGON GPO files with Group Policy Preferences passwords (MS14-025) can significantly accelerate an attacker's lateral movement through a network.

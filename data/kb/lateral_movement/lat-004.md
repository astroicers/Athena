Windows Management Instrumentation (WMI) provides a built-in mechanism for remote process execution that is often less scrutinized than PSExec since it does not create services or write binaries to disk in the same way. Attackers use `wmic.exe` or PowerShell's `Invoke-WmiMethod` to spawn processes on remote hosts using `Win32_Process.Create`, or leverage Impacket's `wmiexec.py` for an interactive-style shell over WMI. WMI execution operates over DCOM (port 135 + dynamic high ports) and leaves fewer artifacts than service-based execution, though process creation events and WMI activity logs (Microsoft-Windows-WMI-Activity/Operational) still provide forensic evidence.
